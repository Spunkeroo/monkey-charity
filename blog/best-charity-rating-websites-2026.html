Before donating to any charity, you should know exactly where your money is going. Not all nonprofits are created equal. Some spend over 95% of every dollar on programs that change lives. Others spend more on executive salaries and fundraising than on the people they claim to serve. The difference between a highly effective charity and a poorly managed one can mean that the same donation produces 100 times more impact or nearly zero impact.

Charity rating websites exist to help donors make informed decisions. They analyze financial data, governance practices, program outcomes, and transparency to give you an objective assessment of how well a nonprofit operates. This guide reviews every major charity evaluation platform available in 2026, explains what each one measures, and shows you exactly how to use them before making your next donation.

Why You Should Check Charity Ratings

The charitable sector in the United States includes over 1.8 million registered nonprofits. While the vast majority are legitimate and well-intentioned, the range of effectiveness is enormous.

Financial mismanagement is real. Some charities spend less than 30% of revenue on programs, with the rest consumed by excessive overhead, fundraising costs, and executive compensation. The FTC and state attorneys general regularly take action against fraudulent charities that collect donations and spend almost nothing on their stated mission.

Effectiveness varies by orders of magnitude. Research from GiveWell demonstrates that the most effective health charities are 100 times more cost-effective than average ones. A dollar donated to the Against Malaria Foundation saves dramatically more lives than a dollar donated to a typical health charity that has not optimized its approach.

Transparency is a leading indicator. Charities that proactively publish financial statements, impact reports, and governance documents tend to be better managed than those that do not. Transparency is not just about accountability. It correlates with organizational quality.

According to the BBB Wise Giving Alliance, donors who research charities before giving are more satisfied with their donations and more likely to become recurring donors. Informed giving creates a positive cycle of engagement.

Charity Navigator

Charity Navigator is the largest and most widely used charity rating platform in the United States, covering over 200,000 nonprofits. Their four-star rating system has become the standard reference for donors evaluating charities.

What They Measure

  • Financial Health (scored 0-100): Revenue growth, program expenses ratio, administrative expenses, fundraising efficiency, working capital ratio, and liabilities-to-assets ratio. Data comes from IRS Form 990 filings.
  • Accountability & Transparency (scored 0-100): Board governance, conflict of interest policy, CEO compensation, donor privacy policy, financial audit, and accessibility of financial documents.
  • Overall Rating (1-4 stars): Combined score from financial health and accountability. Four stars indicates the charity exceeds industry standards.

Strengths

  • Covers the largest number of charities (200,000+)
  • Free to use with no registration required
  • Consistent methodology makes comparison easy
  • Detailed financial breakdowns with charts and trends
  • CEO compensation data for benchmarking

Limitations

  • Does not measure program effectiveness or real-world impact
  • Relies primarily on self-reported financial data
  • Small organizations below revenue thresholds are not rated
  • The overhead ratio is an imperfect proxy for organizational quality

GiveWell

GiveWell takes a fundamentally different approach from Charity Navigator. Instead of rating thousands of charities on financial metrics, GiveWell conducts deep, original research to identify a small number of charities that produce the most impact per dollar donated.

What They Measure

  • Evidence of effectiveness: Has the charity's approach been validated by randomized controlled trials or other rigorous research?
  • Cost-effectiveness: How much impact does each dollar produce compared to other giving opportunities?
  • Room for more funding: Can the charity productively use additional donations?
  • Transparency: Does the organization share detailed data about its operations and outcomes?

Top Charities (2026)

GiveWell currently recommends a small set of top charities including Against Malaria Foundation (malaria prevention nets), GiveDirectly (direct cash transfers), Helen Keller International (vitamin A supplementation), Malaria Consortium (seasonal malaria chemoprevention), and New Incentives (conditional cash transfers for childhood immunization).

Strengths

  • Deepest research methodology of any charity evaluator
  • Focus on cost-effectiveness and real-world impact
  • All research is published transparently, including cost-effectiveness models
  • Has directed over $1.5 billion to top charities since founding

Limitations

  • Only covers a small number of global health and poverty charities
  • Does not rate domestic US charities, environmental organizations, or animal welfare groups
  • Methodology favors easily measurable interventions over systemic change

GuideStar (Candid)

GuideStar, now operating as Candid after merging with the Foundation Center, is the most comprehensive source of nonprofit data in the world. Their database includes information on every IRS-registered nonprofit organization in the United States.

What They Provide

  • Transparency Seals: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum seals based on how much information the nonprofit voluntarily shares. The Platinum Seal of Transparency requires sharing mission, strategy, progress metrics, goals, and financials.
  • Form 990 data: Full IRS filings for every registered nonprofit, including revenue, expenses, executive compensation, and program descriptions.
  • DEI data: Demographic information about board and staff composition for organizations that share it.
  • Program information: Goals, strategies, and metrics that organizations choose to share.

Strengths

  • Covers every registered US nonprofit (1.8 million+)
  • Transparency seals incentivize organizations to share more information
  • Access to raw Form 990 data for independent analysis
  • Free basic access; premium data available for researchers

Limitations

  • Does not provide star ratings or effectiveness evaluations
  • Transparency seals measure disclosure, not quality
  • Data relies on what organizations choose to report

BBB Wise Giving Alliance

The BBB Wise Giving Alliance evaluates nationally soliciting charities against 20 Standards for Charity Accountability. These standards cover governance, financial performance, informational transparency, and solicitation practices.

The 20 Standards

Standards cover areas including: board oversight (independent board, regular meetings, governance policies), spending accountability (at least 65% of expenses on programs, no more than 35% on fundraising), truthful solicitations (accurate representations of programs and finances), and donor privacy (protecting donor information).

Strengths

  • Well-established brand trusted by consumers for over a century
  • Standards are clear, objective, and easy to understand
  • Pass/fail on each of 20 standards provides a simple evaluation

Limitations

  • Covers a relatively small number of charities (approximately 1,300)
  • Does not measure program impact
  • Standards set minimum thresholds rather than identifying best-in-class organizations

Animal Charity Evaluators

Animal Charity Evaluators (ACE) is the leading evaluator of charities focused on animal welfare, applying evidence-based methodology similar to GiveWell's approach to identify the most cost-effective animal charities.

Rating Categories

  • Top Charities: Organizations with the strongest evidence of cost-effective impact on animal suffering. Current top charities include The Humane League, Wild Animal Initiative, and Faunalytics.
  • Standout Charities: Organizations that show promise but may have less evidence or track record than top charities.
  • Comprehensive Reviews: Detailed evaluations of individual organizations covering programs, finances, and impact.

Founders Pledge

Founders Pledge evaluates charities in areas that other evaluators do not cover well, including climate change, mental health, empowerment and equality, and global catastrophic risks. Their research team conducts original analysis to identify the most cost-effective giving opportunities in each cause area.

Key Reports

  • Climate change: Identifies organizations like Clean Air Task Force and Carbon180 as the most cost-effective climate charities based on policy leverage and technology innovation.
  • Mental health: Evaluates global mental health interventions, identifying StrongMinds and Friendship Bench as highly cost-effective.
  • Global health: Provides complementary analysis to GiveWell's research with different methodological assumptions.

IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search

The IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search is the authoritative source for verifying whether an organization is a registered 501(c)(3) and thus eligible to receive tax-deductible donations. This is the first check every donor should make.

What You Can Find

  • Employer Identification Number (EIN)
  • Tax-exempt status and type (501(c)(3), 501(c)(4), etc.)
  • Determination letter dates
  • Whether the organization has had its exempt status revoked
  • Filing requirements and Form 990 history

ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer

ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer provides free access to every Form 990 filed by US nonprofits, with a user-friendly interface that makes it easy to search, view, and download tax filings.

Why It Is Useful

Form 990 is the annual tax filing that nonprofits submit to the IRS. It contains detailed information about revenue, expenses, executive compensation, program descriptions, and governance. ProPublica makes these public documents searchable and presents key data in readable formats. For donors who want to go deeper than star ratings, Form 990 data provides raw financial information for independent analysis.

How to Research a Charity Step by Step

Here is a practical process for evaluating any charity before donating:

  1. Verify legitimacy: Search the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search to confirm 501(c)(3) status. If the organization is not registered, donations are not tax-deductible and the organization may not be legitimate.
  2. Check Charity Navigator: Look for a 3 or 4 star rating. Review the financial health score, program expense ratio, and CEO compensation. A program expense ratio below 65% is a yellow flag.
  3. Check GuideStar: Look for a Gold or Platinum Seal of Transparency. Review the program descriptions, goals, and progress metrics the organization has shared. Limited disclosure is a yellow flag.
  4. Review the charity's own website: Look for published annual reports, audited financial statements, impact metrics, board member list, and a clear description of programs. Legitimate charities make this information readily available.
  5. Search for news and complaints: A quick search for the charity name plus "complaint," "fraud," or "lawsuit" can reveal issues that rating sites may not yet reflect.
  6. Check for impact evaluations: For global health and poverty charities, check GiveWell. For animal charities, check Animal Charity Evaluators. For climate charities, check Founders Pledge. These specialized evaluators go deeper than general platforms.
Red Flags to Watch For

Program expense ratio below 65%. No published financial statements or annual reports. Executive compensation exceeding 2% of total revenue. High-pressure solicitation tactics. Vague descriptions of programs with no measurable outcomes. Organization not registered as a 501(c)(3). Similar names to well-known charities (a common fraud tactic). Refusing to answer questions about finances or programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most reliable charity rating website?

No single website is sufficient for complete charity evaluation. Charity Navigator is best for financial analysis of US nonprofits. GiveWell provides the deepest cost-effectiveness research for global health and poverty charities. GuideStar (now Candid) offers the most comprehensive transparency data. Use at least two sources to get a complete picture of any charity.

Is Charity Navigator accurate?

Charity Navigator is reliable for financial health assessment based on publicly available IRS Form 990 data. Their four-star rating system evaluates financial health, accountability, and transparency. However, financial metrics alone do not capture program effectiveness. A charity can be financially sound but have minimal real-world impact. Pair Charity Navigator with an impact-focused evaluator like GiveWell for a complete picture.

What is the difference between Charity Navigator and GiveWell?

Charity Navigator rates over 200,000 charities on financial health and transparency using publicly available data. GiveWell conducts deep, original research on a small number of charities to identify the most cost-effective giving opportunities. Charity Navigator tells you whether a charity is financially healthy. GiveWell tells you which charities produce the most impact per dollar donated.

How do I check if a charity is a legitimate 501(c)(3)?

Use the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search at irs.gov/charities-non-profits to verify 501(c)(3) status. This free tool searches the IRS database of all registered tax-exempt organizations. You can also verify on GuideStar (Candid), which shows the organization's tax-exempt determination letter, EIN, and filing history.

Should I avoid charities without a Charity Navigator rating?

Not necessarily. Charity Navigator only rates organizations that file IRS Form 990 and meet minimum revenue thresholds. Many legitimate and effective charities, especially smaller ones, religious organizations (which are exempt from filing Form 990), and international organizations may not appear on Charity Navigator. Use GuideStar to verify their tax-exempt status and look for annual reports directly on the charity's website.
