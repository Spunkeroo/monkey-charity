Best Crypto Charities and How to Donate Bitcoin Tax-Free

Donating cryptocurrency to charity is one of the smartest financial moves a crypto holder can make. When you donate appreciated crypto directly to a qualified 501(c)(3) nonprofit, you avoid paying capital gains tax on the appreciation and receive a tax deduction for the full fair market value. It is a double benefit that no other donation method offers.

In 2026, crypto philanthropy has grown into a multi-billion-dollar movement. Major charities now accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, and dozens of other tokens directly. Platforms like The Giving Block have made it as easy as sending a regular transaction. This guide covers how it works, the tax mechanics, the best crypto-native charities, and exactly how to make your first crypto donation.

Why Donating Crypto Is Better Than Donating Cash

To understand the advantage, consider a concrete example. Suppose you bought 1 Bitcoin at $10,000 several years ago and it is now worth $90,000. If you sell and donate the cash:

  1. You sell 1 BTC for $90,000.
  2. You owe long-term capital gains tax on the $80,000 gain. At a 20% federal rate plus 3.8% net investment income tax, that is roughly $19,040 in tax.
  3. You donate the remaining $70,960 (or the full $90,000 and pay tax from other funds).
  4. You receive a charitable deduction for the amount donated.

Now consider donating the Bitcoin directly:

  1. You donate 1 BTC directly to the charity.
  2. You owe zero capital gains tax. The taxable event never occurs.
  3. You receive a charitable deduction for the full $90,000 fair market value.
  4. The charity receives the full $90,000 (they can sell it tax-free as a nonprofit).

In this scenario, donating directly saves you roughly $19,040 in taxes, the charity receives more money, and your deduction is larger. Everyone wins except the IRS.

The Tax Rules for Crypto Donations

Here are the specific IRS rules governing cryptocurrency donations in 2026:

Long-Term Holdings (Held More Than 1 Year)

  • You can deduct the full fair market value of the crypto at the time of donation.
  • You pay zero capital gains tax on the appreciation.
  • The deduction is limited to 30% of your adjusted gross income (AGI) for the year. Excess can be carried forward for up to five years.
  • For donations exceeding $5,000, a qualified appraisal is required and you must file Form 8283 with your tax return.

Short-Term Holdings (Held 1 Year or Less)

  • Your deduction is limited to your cost basis (what you originally paid), not the current market value.
  • You still avoid capital gains tax on the appreciated portion.
  • The deduction limit is 50% of AGI.

Documentation Requirements

  • For donations under $250: keep a written receipt from the charity.
  • For donations $250-$500: written acknowledgment from the charity with the date, amount, and whether any goods or services were received.
  • For donations $500-$5,000: File Form 8283, Section A.
  • For donations over $5,000: File Form 8283, Section B, plus a qualified appraisal within 60 days of the donation.
Pro Tip: Donate Your Biggest Winners

The best crypto to donate is the one with the largest unrealized gain. This maximizes the tax benefit because you avoid the most capital gains tax. After donating, you can immediately repurchase the same crypto with cash if you want to maintain your position (unlike stocks, crypto is not subject to the wash sale rule as of 2026).

Top Crypto Donation Platforms

The Giving Block

The Giving Block is the industry leader in crypto philanthropy infrastructure. Founded in 2018, they process crypto donations for over 2,000 nonprofits including major organizations like Save the Children, UNICEF USA, and the American Cancer Society. They handle the entire process: the donor sends crypto, The Giving Block converts it to USD (or the charity can hold crypto), and the charity receives funds with full tax documentation.

Key features:

  • Supports 100+ cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and major stablecoins.
  • Automatic tax receipts for donors.
  • Donor-Advised Fund (DAF) option for planned giving.
  • Widget that charities can embed on their websites.
  • Zero fees for donors (charities pay a processing fee).

BitPay Donations

BitPay offers crypto payment processing for charities with lower fees than traditional payment processors. They support Bitcoin, Ethereum, and several stablecoins. BitPay instantly converts donations to fiat, protecting charities from volatility.

Engiven

Engiven specializes in crypto donations for churches and faith-based organizations, though they serve all nonprofits. They support over 100 cryptocurrencies and provide real-time conversion with same-day settlement to the charity's bank account.

Direct Wallet Donations

Many crypto-native charities publish their wallet addresses directly. This is the most straightforward method but requires the charity to manage crypto custody themselves. Always verify wallet addresses through official channels to avoid phishing scams.

How to Make Your First Crypto Donation: Step by Step

Method 1: Via The Giving Block

  1. Go to thegivingblock.com and search for your chosen charity.
  2. Click "Donate Crypto" on the charity's profile.
  3. Select the cryptocurrency you want to donate.
  4. Enter the amount and your email (for tax receipt).
  5. Send the crypto to the provided wallet address or scan the QR code.
  6. Receive your tax receipt via email within minutes.

Method 2: Direct Wallet Transfer

  1. Verify the charity's wallet address on their official website (never trust addresses from social media or email).
  2. Open your crypto wallet (hardware wallet recommended for large donations).
  3. Send the crypto to the charity's address.
  4. Save the transaction hash as proof of donation.
  5. Contact the charity for a formal donation receipt for tax purposes.

Method 3: Donor-Advised Fund (DAF)

A crypto DAF allows you to donate crypto, receive an immediate tax deduction, and then distribute grants to charities over time. This is ideal for large crypto holders who want to lock in the tax benefit now but make giving decisions later.

  • Fidelity Charitable: Accepts Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin. $5,000 minimum for crypto contributions.
  • The Giving Block DAF: Accepts 100+ cryptocurrencies. No minimum for contributions.
  • Endaoment: Ethereum-based on-chain DAF with full transparency. Community-governed grant making.

Advanced Strategies for Crypto Philanthropy

Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT)

A CRT allows you to donate appreciated crypto to a trust, receive a partial tax deduction, and then receive income from the trust for a set period (or for life). The remaining assets go to charity. This is a powerful tool for high-net-worth crypto holders who want income and tax benefits while ultimately supporting charity.

Bunching Donations

If your total itemized deductions are close to the standard deduction ($15,700 for single filers in 2026), consider "bunching" multiple years of donations into a single year. For example, instead of donating $5,000 in crypto per year for three years, donate $15,000 in one year. This pushes your itemized deductions above the standard deduction threshold, maximizing the tax benefit.

Donating NFTs and Ordinals

NFTs and Bitcoin Ordinals can also be donated to charity. The IRS treats them as non-cash charitable contributions. For donations of NFTs valued over $5,000, a qualified appraisal is required. This is a niche but growing area of crypto philanthropy, particularly for collectors who hold appreciated digital art.

Red Flags: Avoiding Crypto Charity Scams

The crypto space has its share of bad actors. Here is how to verify a charity before donating:

  • Check Charity Navigator or GuideStar: Look up the organization's rating and financial reports. Use MonkeyCharity to compare transparency scores across multiple evaluation frameworks.
  • Verify 501(c)(3) status: Use the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search tool. Only donations to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations are tax-deductible.
  • Confirm wallet addresses: Always get wallet addresses from the charity's official website, not from social media, email, or third parties.
  • Watch for pressure tactics: Legitimate charities do not pressure you to donate immediately or demand specific cryptocurrencies.
  • Check program spending ratios: At least 70-75% of a charity's budget should go to programs, not administration or fundraising.

The Future of Crypto Philanthropy

Crypto donations are growing faster than any other form of charitable giving. The Giving Block reported that crypto donations grew by over 300% between 2022 and 2025. Several trends are accelerating this growth:

  • On-chain transparency: Blockchain technology allows donors to track exactly how their funds are used, creating a new standard for accountability that traditional charities cannot match.
  • Smart contract giving: Programmable donations that release funds when specific milestones are met, reducing the risk of misuse.
  • Cross-border efficiency: Crypto eliminates the fees and delays of international wire transfers, making it ideal for global humanitarian organizations.
  • Younger donors: Millennials and Gen Z are the most charitable generations in terms of percentage of income donated, and they are also the most likely to hold crypto. The intersection is a massive opportunity.
